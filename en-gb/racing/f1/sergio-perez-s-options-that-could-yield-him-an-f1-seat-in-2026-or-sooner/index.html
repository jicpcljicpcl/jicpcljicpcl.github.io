🏎️ Sergio Perez in High Demand for 2026 F1 Season — Cadillac, Alpine, and Mercedes Among Suitors

Although not racing in the current F1 season, Sergio “Checo” Perez remains a central figure in the driver market as top-tier teams prepare for major shifts in 2026. While Cadillac remains a primary option, Alpine and Mercedes are also in talks with the Mexican driver.

🔑 Key Highlights

  • Cadillac Interest Confirmed:

    • Advanced discussions ongoing with the American team, which sees Perez as a key piece for its 2026 entry.

  • Alpine Talks Resurface:

    • Not Perez’s first link with the team — previously considered when Alpine was Renault.
    • Now under new management (Flavio Briatore), the team is restructuring and sees Perez as a potential marketing and sponsorship boost.
    • His association with major backers like Carlos Slim Domit is a strong asset.

  • Mercedes a Conditional Opportunity:

    • Talks depend on George Russell possibly leaving for Red Bull.
    • Toto Wolff has long admired Perez’s development skills and team spirit.
    • If Max Verstappen leaves Red Bull for Aston Martin, Russell may replace him — opening a seat for Perez.

  • Support & Sponsorship:

    • Despite some sponsor losses post-Red Bull, Checo still holds major personal backing from influential figures like Slim.

📅 Outlook for 2026

Perez’s next move depends on driver dominoes, particularly Verstappen and Russell. Whether as a team leader at Cadillac or a strategic second driver at Mercedes, Perez’s future remains a hot topic in the F1 paddock.
